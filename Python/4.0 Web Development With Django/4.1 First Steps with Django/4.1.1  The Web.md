**What is the Web?**

Before speaking about what the Web truly _is_, we must first understand how it was born. Because its genesis reveals its deepest purpose and the immense power it holds over our lives today.

In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee — a physicist working at CERN, the very place where the Higgs boson would later be discovered — faced a critical problem: scientists were producing thousands of documents, yet each one lived in total isolation. Different machines, different formats, no way to connect them. Knowledge was trapped.

So he asked a revolutionary question: **“What if we created a single, universal web where all human knowledge could live together and speak to each other?”**

That question gave birth to the first web server on Christmas Day, 1990.

In that same year, Berners-Lee gave the world three sacred inventions that still form the soul of everything we build with Django today:

1. **HTML** — HyperText Markup Language The language that gives structure and meaning to information. With HTML we say to a document: “This is a title. This is a paragraph. This is a link.” The magic word is **HyperText** — text that goes _beyond_ itself. A book is linear. The Web is not. Any page can point to any other page. That single idea changed everything.
2. **HTTP** — HyperText Transfer Protocol The language in which machines converse. A protocol is simply an agreed set of rules. HTTP is the grammar of the request-response dance: the browser asks, the server answers. Every time you open a website, you are starting a polite, universal conversation.
3. **URL** — Uniform Resource Locator The universal address system. Without URLs, no document could ever find another across millions of machines. Thanks to the URL, https://nasa.gov means exactly the same thing whether you are in Mérida, Mexico or in Tokyo, Japan.

The impact of the Web is almost impossible to put into words. We now have near-infinite access to knowledge from any corner of the planet. A biologist in Asia can speak instantly with one in Europe. The servers of Facebook, Instagram, YouTube — and even the databases behind the AI models we talk to every day — all live on this Web. Banking systems, governments, and scientific research all flow through it.

**Think of it spiritually:** The Web is the externalization of human consciousness itself. It is humanity’s collective mind made visible — a digital Indra’s Net where every page is a jewel reflecting every other jewel.

Thanks to the Web, you can read the words of people who lived before Christ. Translations have become almost instantaneous because language itself has become global. The technological evolution of our species has exploded.

The Web is not just technology. **The Web is the collective human soul being woven in real time** — by every curious being who dares to leave a conscious mark on this infinite network of knowledge.
